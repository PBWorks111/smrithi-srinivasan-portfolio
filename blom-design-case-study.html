Project Context

Our client wanted us to streamline and redesign two websites to make them more user friendly. They wanted to update their main website to add COVID-19 information and they also wanted us to integrate their new ordering platform. This section covers the user designs that were completed.

Problem

Design a solution to streamline both website platforms

Solution

Use findings to redesign both websites based on user painpoints

Design Process

Step 1

Analyze ux research and decide how to design app

Step 2

Redesign website

Step 3

Conduct usability test and implement feedback

Project Scoping

Initially, our client had proposed three projects we could choose from. Due to our timeline, we could not complete every single project and we decided to focus on two of the projects.

  • The first project was to find a way to allow customers to make indoor reservations and prepay their beverages/food. Ideally, the goal was to organize everything through their point of sales
  • The second project was reorganizing the client’s websites to highlight their delivery, curbside and indoor patio seating options. At the time, the client had felt that their website’s flow was clunky and the information was not clear.

Unfortunately, due to rising COVID-19 cases, Michigan placed a law stating that indoor dining was not allowed. We still went ahead with both projects. However, we kept the new laws in mind as we changed the website.

Wireframe Design

After research, our team begun wireframe creation by dividing into two groups. One focused on the Squarespace-based main website. Team members A, C, and I focused the Square-powered ordering page. My role involved understanding Square's platform and helping out with mid-fidelity mockup development. Initially, we crafted low-fidelity mockups for both platforms' navigation bars, approved by the client.

We also focused on mid-fidelity mockups. My task was to verify the design feasibility within Square's limitations. After creating mid-fidelity mockups for both websites, the team, excluding myself, finalized wireframes. I ensured alignment with Square's capabilities. Finally, members A and C integrated all wireframes into a prototype.

Usability Testing

After creating the final wireframes, we decided to do last-minute user testing. We got six people to do an unmoderated usability test of our prototype. Our team found that:

Customers were able to find important information like the order page and menu

Customers felt confused about the “Merchandise” page that was added

Customers felt slightly uncomfortable jumping between pages on the prototype
