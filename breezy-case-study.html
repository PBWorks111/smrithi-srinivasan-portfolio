Breezy the Beaver- The To Do List

Project Context

My supervisor created a chatbot named Breezy with an eager beaver character to assist users in managing their to-do lists. I gathered feedback from 20 to 30 users to understand their experiences and suggestions for improving Breezy.

Problem

Find out who main users were and their preferences towards Breezy.

Solution

Conduct user experience research to understand who the users were and what their preferences were.

Clickstream Analysis

In this part, we looked at info about who uses the app and how they used it. From 29 users, we found most are female and from the US, with others from places like France and Brazil. Many speak English, but also French, Portuguese, or Dutch.

From 29 users, we found most are female and from the US, with others from places like France and Brazil.

Users often use the to-do list and regularly add tasks. They also quickly try out different parts of the chatbot, showing they understand it fast. But we also found some things that could be better.

Users preferred tapping options on Facebook Messenger to add tasks, rather than typing them out.

Users disliked receiving daily reminders

While users liked Breezy, they desired more interaction and complexity. They wanted her to respond to various comments and messages.

Users expressed a desire to respond to messages or schedule appointments

Survey

Following the clickstream analysis, I conducted a survey with previous users over three weeks. Despite only six previous users responding, we combined their feedback with responses from five new users in our unmoderated usability test to get more results.

We asked our previous users to rate Breezy’s personality on a scale of 1 to 10, 1 being that she has a terrible personality while 10 means that she is very friendly. Previous users rated Breezy's personality as 7/10, while new users rated it as 8.4/10. Most users described her as cute and fun, though two saw her more as a to-do list.

We also asked previous users how they would describe Breezy in a few short words. Among new users, 3 out of 5 found her to be a lot of fun. We also asked how easy it was to use Breezy’s features non a scale of 1 to 10. Both previous and new users found her features easy to use, with scores averaging 9.33/10 and 9.6/10, respectively.

Finally, we asked users what Breezy’s most memorable features were. New users appreciated the ability to prioritize tasks, while previous users liked the daily reminders.

Unmoderated Usability Test

After conducting the surveys on previous users, I went ahead and conducted an unmoderated users on the service, trymyui.com with new users. For this section, we went ahead and conducted 5 unmoderated usability testing sessions with new users who used facebook often and who used to-do lists often. It should be noted that these users between their early 20s and early 40s.

Image of Trymyui on Facebook Messenger

All users managed to use the add task button, yet some preferred typing tasks directly.

Users easily deleted tasks, rearranged tasks, and accessed the settings page, but 2 users disliked horizontal scrolling for menu options on Facebook Messenger.

When asked to tell a joke, two users struggled initially but eventually figured out they had to click an automated response for further feedback.

After usability testing, we asked about users' experience with other to-do list apps and their familiarity with Breezy's counterparts. Here were the findings:

Most new users used conventional to-do lists, with only one familiar with a Breezy-like app

Users purchased apps for self-improvement or entertainment

Users suggested expanding Breezy's reminder features and integrating with software like Siri or Google Calendar

Finally, we also asked users to complete the same survey questions we asked our previous users. The results for the survey questions are listed in the previous section.

Final Recommendations

After my research, I drafted recommendations for immediate implementation to my supervisor. Firstly, my supervisor and I decided to give the welcome message clearer instructions for users to understand Breezy's functions easily. Secondly, we expanded the chatbot's personality by incorporating personalized responses on topics based on user feedback.

Personalized Responses for Breezy

Initially, I suggested changing the reminders system either by removing it entirely or offering further customization options. However, due to limitations with Manychat, it wasn’t feasible. Limitations

I encountered several limitations during this project. Firstly, the low response rate to my survey - only 6 from old users and 5 from new users – made it statistically insignificant even though there was a three-week wait for more responses. Another consideration is that I was given advice to reach out to users who used potential competitors of Breezy to understand their thoughts about her. However, at the time, my supervisor and I only knew of one competitor.There was also the possibility of bias in responses from previous users who may have been friends or family of employees. Finally, , our user selection wasn't restricted to one-time Breezy users. We tried to be inclusive as possible to get more users.

Conclusion

This project brought me full circle, returning to the company that introduced me to UX/UI. I learned Breezy's unique personality and expanded its potential. It rekindled my interest in chatbots and user interaction with AI. As my first solo UX research project, it was a valuable learning experience. Moving forward, I aim to stay in AI and explore beyond chatbots..
