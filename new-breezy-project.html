User Experience Research for To-Do List Chatbot

Facebook Chatbot Messenger

Company: Traits A.I


Project Context

My supervisor created a chatbot called Breezy to help users keep track of their to-do list with the help of cute, eager beaver named Breezy. At the time, she had about 20 to 30 users who tried her out. My supervisor wanted me to reach out to the users to understand their overall experience with Breezy. They wanted to learn about who their users were, what they liked and disliked about Breezy and how she could be improved upon.

Problem
Find out who main users were and their preferences towards Breezy.
Solution
Conduct user experience research to understand who the users were and what their preferences were
Design Process
Project Scoping
For the first half of my project, I had to figure out what type of user research I wanted to conduct. I came up with three proposals for my supervisor and together we narrowed down on one proposal. In the end, we decided to create a clickstream analysis, a customer satisfaction survey and a moderated interview session.
Clickstream Analysis
For this section, we focused on gathering data from current users and learned about their demographics, when they first chatted with her and their native languages. After reviewing 29 subscribers, we found that that more than half the users were female. We also learned that a majority of our users were from the United States.The remaining users were from France, Brazil, Indonesia and other nations respectively. Also, most of our users were English speakers, but they also spoke other languages like French, Portuguese and Dutch.

When users used the app, we found that most users the to-do list very often and felt the need to add tasks to their list. We also noticed that users spent a time testing different parts of the chatbot for short periods of time. This was the first sign we saw that indicated users were able to grasp Breezy pretty quickly. Although we learned a lot about users, we also noticed a few painpoints as well.
  • Users had to tap an option on Facebook Messenger to add a task instead of typing out their task in the message box
  • Users also did not like receiving daily reminders
  • Users also seemed to like Breezy about, but they wanted to interact with her more and she wasn’t a complex AI who could respond to different comments and messages .
  • Users wanted to be able to respond to messages or set appointments
    • Survey
    After the clickstream analysis, I conducted a survey with new and previous users to further understand their thoughts and feelings when they interact with Breezy. The survey was conducted over a series of 3 weeks. Unfortunately, within those 3 weeks, only 6 of our previous users responded. Even though we were unable to create a fully-fledged survey, we were able to combine our results from the survey we provided to 5 new users. These were the results from both surveys:
  • We asked our previous users to rate Breezy’s personality on a scale of 1 to 10, 1 being that she has a terrible personality while 10 means that she is very friendly. On average, previous users rate Breezy’s personality 7/10. For new users, we asked them to rate her personality on a scale of 1 to 7, 1 being that she had a terrible personality while 10 meant she had a nice personality. New users rated her personality 8.4/10.
  • We also asked previous users how they would describe Breezy in a few short words. A majority of users felt that she was really cute and fun. Two of our previous users felt that she was more of a to-do list in general. We asked this same question to our 5 new users and 3/5 of users found her to be a lot of fun.
  • We also asked how easy it was to use Breezy’s features non a scale of 1 to 10. 1 meant that Breezy’s features were not easy to use while 10 meant her features were easy to use. Our previous users gave an average score of 9.33/10 while our new users gave a score of 9.6/10.
  • Finally, we asked users what Breezy’s most memorable features were. In this case, >new users liked being able to prioritize tasks and control their daily reminders while old users liked seeing the daily reminders in general.
    • Unmoderated User Interviews

    After conducting the surveys on previous users, I went ahead and conducted an unmoderated users on the service, trymyui.com.

    For this section, we went ahead and conducted 5 unmoderated usability testing sessions with new users who used facebook often and who used to-do lists often. It should be noted that these users between their early 20s and early 40s. An unmoderated testing session is when users record themselves completing tasks. User researchers/ moderators are not available during the session. We asked the users to complete a series of tasks using Breezy. These were the results we found:

  • All users were able to use the add task button, but a few users wished that they could just type out their task
  • Users were easily able to delete tasks, move tasks to the top of the list and even use the settings page. However, at least 2 users did not like how they had to scroll horizontally to see all of the menu options.
  • When users were asked to tell Breezy a joke, two users found it difficult to tell a joke. They were able to ask Breezy to tell a joke, but they did not know that they had to click on an automated response to get further feedback

    • After the usability test, we asked the new users questions about other apps to-do list apps they’ve used in general and whether they were similar to Breezy the Beaver to see if they were familiar with other to-do list apps like Breezy. These were some of our main findings:

  • New users often used regular to-do lists and only one user has seen an app similar to Breezy
  • Users bought apps for two different reasons, self-improvement or entertainment purposes
  • Users also mentioned that they felt that Breezy could be expanded to handle additional reminder features and they also wanted additional integration features with other software like Siri or Google Calendar

    • Finally, we also asked users to complete the same survey questions we asked our previous users. The results for the survey questions are listed in the previous section.

    Final Recommendations

    After completing my research, I created a list of potential recommendations that could be implemented immediately. The first action my supervisor and I decided to enact was create new welcome message should be created to include more instructions/ explanations. This was created to help explain Breezy’s functions early on while making sure the instructions were convenient for users to come back and visit at any point. The second action that was taken on was expanding the chatbot’s personality by adding more personalized responses for different topics. The topics chosen were based on users’ feedback.


    I initially recommended changing the reminders system to remove altogether or to further customize it for all users. Unfortunately, Manychat, the tool used to build Breezy, is currently incapable of integrating an advanced technical feature like this. The final action I made was to create a set of user personas to help describe the potential user as a whole my supervisor and I had no idea who our main users were at the time of research.





    Limitations

    There were a number of limitations I faced throughout this project. The first limitation I faced first was from the number of responses I received for my survey. I only got 6 responses from old users and 5 responses from new users and this made the survey statistically insignificant. I waited three weeks for potential responses from previous users and I couldn’t get enough responses.



    Another consideration is that I was given advice to reach out to users who used potential competitors of Breezy to understand their thoughts about her. However, at the time, my supervisor and I only knew of one competitor. Also, some of the previous users may have been friends or family members of the company and their responses for using the product would have been slightly biased. Finally, we never narrowed our spectrum of users to people who just used Breezy one time. Instead, we tried to be as inclusive as possible to get as much data as we could.


    Conclusion

    This project allowed me to come first circle as I was able to come back to the first company that introduced me to UX/UI. Working and learning about Breezy was a unique experience as I had to learn about her quirks and find a way to grow them to further develop her personality. Working on Breezy also reintroduced me to the world of chatbots and their expansive personalities. It also allowed me to learn more about how users interacted with different types of AI. While this project was the first solo UX research I conducted, it was a project that taught me so much about how to conduct different types of UX research. Going forward, I hope to stay within the field of AI and learn more about different types of AI that exist outside of chatbots. If I were to do this project over again, I would try to work around a longer timeline and include more research to narrow down on users’ particular needs and desires.
