HR Employment App- By George HR

Project Context

My supervisor aimed to create an all-in-one app focused on employment laws regarding sexual harassment, enabling users to anonymously report incidents.

Problem

Existing resources were scattered: employment law info hard to find, & few anti-workplace harassment apps.

Solution

Create a mobile app to protect employees.

Survey

At the beginning of this project, I reached out to 28 people, who were currently working and were over the age of 18, and asked them the following types of questions.

The apps they generally used

Their demographics

The type of companies they worked for

Their knowledge about laws surrounding harassment

How open and easy it is for employees to communicate their thoughts

How important prevention is at their company

How often they thought about sexual harassment

Here were some of the findings:

Job security and open communication: Users typically worked in secure environments with open communication.

Industry focus: App usage aligned with users' professions

Limited legal knowledge: Awareness of sexual harassment laws was generally low.

Low harassment awareness: Most users rarely considered harassment

Usability Testing and Second Iteration of Design

After the initial design, I paused the process for personal reasons and completed the app in August 2020 with one round of usability testing. I tested it with four people, gathering feedback on features and aesthetics. Here are the results.

  • At least two users did not like the app's aesthetics
  • All of the users understood every feature of the app

    Limitations

    Several points to note during the design process: The survey respondents, friends and family of my colleagues, might have been biased. I only considered sexual harassment prevention apps in my competitor research, excluding employment apps. Additionally, any changes in sexual harassment prevention laws over the past one to two years were not reflected in the app.

    Conclusion

    This was my first UX design project, and I learned a lot. I discovered the importance of continuous iteration, as high-fidelity mockups always needed tweaks. Complexity and ambiguity can confuse users, so clarity and clear intent are crucial.

    I also realized that design and experience are interconnected. Initially focused on functionality, I saw the need for design improvements only after usability testing. Balancing user experience and design depends on user preferences.

    The next step is another round of user testing to catch any missed aspects and update for any recent changes in laws.
