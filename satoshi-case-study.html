Nakamoto Research

I researched Nakamoto's background and his personality and I summarized all the facts I had. While I didn’t create a persona during the project, I created one for my portfolio based on those findings.

Psychology Research

I researched response times to virtual messages. I found that text messages typically get a reply within 90 seconds, with complex topics taking longer. Users expect quicker responses to texts than emails or voicemails. 1 in 5 people expect Twitter replies within 15 minutes, showing a demand for instant messages.

Development/Design

My supervisor decided to create a quiz through the chatbot. Those who passed were offered a prize. The quiz started with beginner questions and became more difficult. There were three levels to complete. The Satoshi Nakamoto chatbot grew more suspicious with each wrong answer but gradually opened up as users succeeded. A user flow diagram below shows the entire bot.

User Flow Diagram of Bot

I wanted to make Nakamoto as human as possible by emphasizing his suspicious and secretive nature. My supervisor and I also created a question bank for users to ask personal questions if they failed the quiz. This was inspired by other chatbots' and their engagement with their users. I timed the chatbot's responses to mimic natural typing pauses.

This is an image of final result of the chatbot during the design/development phase.

The chatbot was built using Chatfuel, an online platform that lets users interact with a chatbot by selecting options. A keyword databank was used to personalize responses.

Testing the Chatbot

I tested my design on three of my colleagues. All of my colleagues were interns who had very little to no knowledge about Bitcoin. The Bitcoin founder was tested for two criteria: how interactive he was and how human he was:

The chatbot felt the most human was when typing bubbles pop up while it was typing out its responses and throughout his suspicious side.

His suspicious nature helped bring out his personality

One of the main things that could be changed was the length of his responses. Even though I was able to time the chatbot’s responses, my colleagues felt tired out by the lengthy sets of paragraphs

Discussion

Although I wasn't the user researcher or designer, I researched Nakamoto's personality and created a mental persona that was similar to a user persona. I also tested the bot on three colleagues, but their responses are biased as they don’t have Bitcoin knowledge. Usually, user testing would involve five people who are familiar with Bitcoin..

Conclusion

Creating the chatbot taught me how to create and replicate someone’s personality. It was also my first experience testing a product with multiple users.
