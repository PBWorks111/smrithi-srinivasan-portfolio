Project Context

For my consumer health course, my final project was focused on creating an app for underrepresented individuals that helps them with their health. My group decided to create an app for recently released convicts. However, we needed to understand what our users needed from this app. After conducting a literature review and 3 interviews, we designed an app based on their needs.


Problem

Understand what recently released convicts needed to help make their transition back into society as easy for them as possible.

Solution

Create an app that would help recently released prisoners with their personal goals.

UX Design Process

Communicated with stakeholders on each use case. Figured out which use cases to keep. Some use cases were meant for older products

Project Scoping

In the first half of this project, we reviewed recent academic studies (2015-2018) on recidivism. We explored its definition, affected populations, impacts, and current interventions. Here's what we learned.

Most people incarcerated were male between the ages of 18 to 25

People often suffered from mental illnesses like PTSD, social alienation, etc

People who were incarcerated often completed secondary education/GED

People who had a mental illness were more likely to get incarcerated again

Once we had a basic understanding of who our user was and how life in prison effected them, we went on to interview ex-convicts to further understand their personal experiences and their life after incarceration in the state of Michigan. Below are the interview questions two of my group members and I created.

Can you tell me a little about your current job?

What were the challenges you faced after being released from jails? What were the biggest challenges you faced when looking for employment after release?

What types of mental health resources were available for returning prisoners at that time? How did you access those mental health resources? What mental health resources did you use after being released from jails? How effective/helpful were the resources to your mental health conditions?

Any challenges you faced when finding mental health treatments you need? Any obstacles you experienced when receiving mental health treatments? How important is accessing mental health resources to you?

How do you communicate with your caseworker? What do they talk about? How often do they talk? Method of communication?

What technology, if any, have you used to take care of your mental health? How effective are they? What obstacles did you experience when using such technology?

Due to our timeline, my group and I had to begin ideating features for the app before we finished all of our interviews. We relied on the academic studies and one interview for this part. Based on the criteria needed for our project, our research and one of our users’ thoughts, we decided including the following features for our app.

Therapy sessions to promote better mental health

Interview Results

After my group and I ideated on potential features, we were able to finish our last two interviews and get a grand total of three interviews. Here is what we learned from all three interviews.

There weren’t any guides or pamphlets to help people recently released in Michigan.

They had to rely on personal support systems or face homelessness and halfway homes.

Many struggled to find jobs or mental health resources. While there were job resources like Michigan Works, mental health resources were lacking.

Older ex-prisoners had lower technology literacy than younger ones

Many dealt with mental illness both inside and outside prison, with long term consequences like anxiety, overstimulation, depression, alcoholism, and drug addiction.

User Personas

As we were figuring out which features we wanted to include on our app, we were also asked to make two personas of our users. Here were the personas that we made.

Persona 1- Laquan Reed

Persona 2- Wade Warren

Scenario

Laquan was recently released from prison after 8 years. He’s currently living with his family and is also struggling with his mental health issues. Laquan feels like he needs help with dealing with his mental health and his drug addiction. as it’s begun to take a toll on his family. He’s begun lashing out at his family members and he feels really bad about it. His wife has tried talking to him, but he feels like she doesn’t understand what he’s been through. One of his caseworkers recommended the “RecidiVision” app to find free resources to help him deal with his PTSD and to get connect with other prisoners who understand what he’s been through. He goes to his phone and looks up the app. From there, he finds an option to find a therapist based on his needs. From there he gets a list of recommended therapists he can visit. He also finds a feature that shows events and workshops hosted by established releasees and decides to go to one to find a community for himself.

Wade is 26 years old and was just released from prison. He currently has no money and no job and he’s having trouble navigating life outside of prison. He wants to get his life back on track but he doesn’t know what his options are given his criminal record. One day, he learns about the “RecidiVision” app through one of his ex-releasee friends. Wade downloads the app on this phone and finds a feature that allows him to watches videos that teach him vocational skills(like job interviews and creating a resume) and technical skills (like Microsoft Office, online tools, etc). He begins watching the videos and applying everything he learns by practicing using his local library’s computers. He also finds a meditation function that teaches him about mindfulness and breathing techniques to help control his anger so that he can manage his stress and effectively find a job for himself as soon as possible.

App Design

As a group, we understood what features we wanted. However, we needed to expand on how we wanted these features to function for two reasons. First, we all needed In this case, we wanted to focus on three different areas. First, we decided to narrow down on our design constraints to make sure we understood our limitations for our design.

Not everyone had access to high speed internet once they were released from incarceration. Since our app requires some data, we decided to create our app for smartphones that used a minimum of 3G

App Monitoring - We needed to make sure that we figured out a way to monitor who would be conducting community building events

Funding for Therapists- Health insurance can be expensive for people and our goal was to find therapists or government funded therapists who could help our users. We recognized that it takes time to find the right therapist that works for them.

Maintaining a simpler design style to make sure users are not overstimulated from app design.

Once we knew our design constraints, we went ahead and narrowed down how each of the app’s features would function. Here is what we came up with.

Therapist Matching- Match people with a therapist based on their needs by providing a quiz to narrow down a list of potential therapists based on their needs. Users can look at the profile of the therapist and they can schedule a meeting with them or start chatting.

Peer to Peer Weekly Mental Health Support Group- There would be mentors who could facilitate different events virtually and there would be multiple group sessions a week. Every week, a different topic would be chosen by the mentor. Between 6 to 10 users can attend one event

Educational Resources- Users would be shown videos on different skillsets like interviewing for jobs, building a resume, technical skills like Microsoft Word, etc.

After we decided on the functionality of each feature, we divided our list up. I was in charge of creating the therapist matching function. First, all of us created sketches on what these functions would look like, then we created low fidelity mockups, and finally we created mid- to high- fidelity mockups to show what the final product could look like. Limitations/Discussions

Given our timeline, we couldn't cover every stage of the design process. With more time, we would have conducted additional interviews, created an affinity wall, and used other UX research methods, like surveys, to understand the experiences of recently released convicts. We also didn't conduct usability testing. Limited by time, we only created one set of designs but would have preferred to go through two or three design iterations.

Conclusion

This project highlighted the lack of resources for supporting life after incarceration. While many apps focus on global issues, we often forget about local needs. Though this app wasn't fully developed, I hope to expand on it in the future as a side project.
